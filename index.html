About The Club

Welcome to the JCHS chapter of the TED-Ed club! Believe it or not, the very first TED Talk conference was held back in 1984. The founder, Richard Saul Wurman, wanted a place to discuss the three fields of technology, entertainment, and design--hence the name, TED. The millions of viral videos we’ve seen today, from “Do Schools Kill Creativity” to “Inside The Mind of a Master Procrastinator” led TED to expand beyond just one conference, but also to speakers across the globe. As a member of this club, you will have the chance to practice your public speaking skills, engage with your peers, and share your own “idea worth spreading” at a mini TEDEd conference in the end of the school year. Have a brilliant idea you want to explore and share with the world? Join today!

Schedule

  • 13 Explorations

Each meeting is called an Exploration, and there are 13 in total. Below is the name of each Exploration and a brief description of what happens.

Exploration 1

Tell Us Who You Are

This Exploration is all about getting to know each other and identifying and articulating the ideas that motivate and inspire us.

Exploration 2

Discover Great Ideas

In this Exploration, we will discuss, consider and define the important qualities of an “idea worth spreading”.

Exploration 3

Learn the Art of Feedback

This Exploration provides a feedback framework we will use throughout the following Explorations.

Exploration 4

Identify Your Ideas

In this Exploration, we will explore our possible topics more deeply and practice giving and receiving feedback.

Exploration 5

Build Your Throughline

A throughline is the main connecting theme or anchoring idea in a story. Every Talk should have a throughline that is clearly identifiable throughout. This Exploration will help establish that anchor.

Exploration 6

Plan Your Talk Arc

During this Exploration, we will identify the key elements of a great story and map out the path from beginning to middle to end.

Exploration 7

Research and Support Your Idea

In this Exploration, we will think about what we have learned about story arcs, and decide how to support our ideas with evidence.

Exploration 8

Write Your Script

In this Exploration, we will bring together a draft of our throughline, storyboard, and research and will have time to develop our script.

Exploration 9

Make It Visual

In this Exploration, we will identify when and how to use visuals and how to create truly powerful visuals.

Exploration 10

Say Your Talk Out Loud

In this Exploration, we will become comfortable sharing out loud what we’ve developed.

Exploration 11

Practice, Edit, Practice

In this Exploration, we will rehearse our Talk, receive and incorporate feedback, and then rehearse some more! We will practice building a connection with our audience.

Exploration 12

Final Rehearsal

In this Exploration, we will hold a dress rehearsal to help work out last-minute kinks in our scripts and performances.

Exploration 13

Record the Talk

We will use our visuals, public speaking skills, and research to present our ideas worth spreading. This is the big day for our group!

F.A.Q

  • What is TED-Ed?

    TED-Ed is the youth version of TED. According to the official website, “TED-Ed’s mission is to spark and celebrate the ideas of teachers and students around the world.”

  • When are the meetings?

    Every other Thursday from 3:30-4:30.

  • What are the requirements to join?

    All grades and levels of experience are welcome! You just need the signed permission form handed out at our first meeting.

  • What if I can’t think of any interesting topics?

    Speeches can range from a broad set of topics--from informative to entertaining to persuasive! The first several meetings will be focused on learning about our ideas and interests, sharing ideas with peers, and narrowing down the choices to get your own personal topic. We will also take a look at previous examples from other clubs.

  • What can I get out of this club?

    Joining this club will help you improve your public speaking skills and learn how to work and share with others. You will also give a final speech at the last meeting about a topic you are passionate about, and will have explored throughout the year. This speech can be recorded and used for college applications, your own personal memory, or to share on the TED-Ed Student Talks Youtube channel for the world to see!
